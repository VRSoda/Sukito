Privacy Policy

Sukito · Last updated: April 2026

Sukito is a desktop calendar widget for Windows. This policy explains what data the app accesses and how it is handled.

Data We Access

When you connect your Google account, Sukito requests access to:

  • Google Calendar — to read and write your calendar events

How Your Data Is Used

  • Calendar events are fetched and displayed within the app on your device
  • Events you create or modify in the app are written back to your Google Calendar
  • No data is sent to any third-party server — all processing happens locally on your machine

Data Storage

  • OAuth tokens (used to stay logged in) are stored locally in the app's data folder on your device
  • Calendar data is not persisted beyond what is cached temporarily in memory
  • Your API keys (e.g. OpenWeatherMap) are stored locally in your browser's localStorage and never transmitted

Data Sharing

Sukito does not share, sell, or transmit your personal data to any external parties. The only external services the app communicates with are:

  • Google Calendar API — to sync your events
  • OpenWeatherMap API — to fetch local weather data
  • ip-api.com — to detect your approximate location for weather (no account or personal data required)

Revoking Access

You can disconnect your Google account at any time from the app's Settings screen. You can also revoke access from your Google Account permissions page.

Contact

If you have any questions about this policy, please open an issue on GitHub.
